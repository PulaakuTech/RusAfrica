About

Rostov State Medical University, founded in 1930 on the basis of the Medical Faculty of the Warsaw Russian University, is located in the picturesque city center. Its campus includes educational departments, clinics, a sports complex, student dormitories, a canteen and a student clinic.

The university has its own clinical base designed for 538 beds, including specialized centers such as Neurology, Cardiology, Eye Microsurgery, Anesthesiology, and more. The NIIAP Clinic includes 9 inpatient departments with 330 beds.

RostSMU employs 880 highly qualified teachers, including 82 professors and 151 doctors of science. Since 1961, more than 12,610 international students have graduated from its preparatory faculty.

Today, 1,038 foreign citizens study at RostSMU, with the largest numbers from India, Ecuador, Lebanon, Palestine, and Nigeria.

Rostov-on-Don is the largest city in Southern Russia with a population of over 1.1 million. It has a mild winter climate, affordable living, and excellent infrastructure for students.

Accommodation

International students live in comfortable dormitories No.1 and No.2 located at the heart of the city. These five-story buildings are equipped with triple rooms, internet, hygienic facilities, and catering options.

Nearby facilities include clinics, shops, pharmacies, cafes, and public transport. Dormitory No.3 accommodates 457 students with upgraded facilities and proximity to hospitals.

Students also benefit from self-study rooms, a 144-seat dining hall, safety orientation, and a student council. Dormitories are disability-friendly and close to libraries and cultural spots.

Cost of accommodation: 1294 rubles/month.
